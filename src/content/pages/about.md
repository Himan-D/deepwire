Lipi is a template for Astro, built for writers who care about how their words look on the page. It is not a general-purpose blog theme, and it is not trying to be. It is a publishing environment for essays, travel notes, developer journals, and personal archives: the kind of writing that benefits from a considered layout and a quiet interface.

The design is called Kami, after the Japanese word for paper. It begins with a warm parchment ground and a type scale built around Literata, a serif face designed for reading at length. The measure is constrained. The spacing is generous. The interface stays out of the way. Lipi builds to static HTML with minimal JavaScript, and the typography holds under Cmd+P.

## The Localhost Studio

The Localhost Studio is a small independent studio building thoughtful tools for writers and publishers. We work at the intersection of typography, publishing, and the web, with a preference for things that are made carefully and maintained honestly.

Lipi began as a personal template, became a design exploration, and eventually became something we thought was worth sharing. The Kami design language runs through everything we make: the same warmth, the same restraint, the same conviction that the content should come first.
